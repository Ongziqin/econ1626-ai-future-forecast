# AI and Australia’s Economy (2027–2030) — Economic Forecast Web Essay

## Overview
This project is an interactive web-based economic forecast analyzing how artificial intelligence may reshape the Australian economy between 2027 and 2030. It combines economic theory, scenario modelling, and data visualisation to explore productivity, labour markets, and policy responses.

The project is inspired by AI forecasting approaches and applies economic frameworks such as:
- Solow Growth Model (TFP-driven growth)
- Task-based Labour Economics (Autor)
- Endogenous Growth Theory

## Key Features

### 📊 Interactive Data Visualisation
- GDP growth scenario chart (Baseline / Upside / Downside)
- Labour market automation vs augmentation model

### 🧠 Economic Analysis
- AI as a general-purpose technology (GPT)
- Productivity and total factor productivity (TFP) modelling
- Task-based labour market restructuring

### 🏛️ Policy Analysis
- Short, medium, and long-term policy framework
- Trade-offs between innovation, regulation, and inequality

### ⚠️ Uncertainty Handling
- Structural uncertainty in AI forecasting
- Measurement limitations in productivity data
- Institutional variation across economies

## Methodology & Tools
- HTML5 / CSS3 for structure and design
- JavaScript (Chart.js) for data visualisation
- Economic theory synthesis from academic literature
- Scenario-based forecasting approach

---

## Key Assumptions
- AI adoption increases gradually from 2027–2030
- Productivity gains depend on diffusion speed and policy response
- Labour impacts are task-based, not job-based
- Outcomes are probabilistic, not deterministic
