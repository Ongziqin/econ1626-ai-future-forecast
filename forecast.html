Australia's AI Economy 2030

An Interactive Economic Forecast

Thesis

Artificial intelligence is becoming one of the most influential technologies in modern economic history. This forecast argues that by 2030 generative AI will significantly increase Australia's productivity and economic output. However, the benefits will not be distributed equally. Without effective policies in education, competition and workforce transition, AI may increase labour market inequality and concentrate economic power among large firms. The future impact of AI will therefore depend not only on technological progress but also on policy choices made during the next five years.

AI Capabilities and Economic Mechanisms

Economists classify AI as a potential general-purpose technology similar to electricity and the internet. AI creates economic impact through four major channels.

  • Task Automation – AI automates routine cognitive work.
  • Productivity Growth – workers can produce more output in less time.
  • Innovation – new products and services emerge.
  • Market Structure Changes – large firms may gain stronger competitive advantages.

Joseph Schumpeter described innovation as creative destruction, where new technologies continuously replace older ways of producing goods and services. AI may become the next major example of this process.

Scenario Analysis
