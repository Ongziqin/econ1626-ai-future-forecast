AI and the Australian Economy (2027–2030): A Scenario Forecast

Thesis

Artificial Intelligence will act as a general-purpose technology reshaping Australia’s economy through productivity gains, labour substitution in routine cognitive tasks, and structural shifts across mining, services, education, and healthcare. By 2030, outcomes depend heavily on adoption speed, policy response, and labour market adaptation.

Economic Mechanisms

  • Productivity Shock: AI reduces marginal cost of cognitive labour
  • Task Automation: 20–40% of tasks in services exposed
  • Wage Polarisation: High-skill workers gain, mid-skill roles compressed
  • Capital Shift: Investment flows into compute + data infrastructure

GDP Forecast Scenarios

Employment Transition

Scenario Summary

Baseline: Gradual adoption, +1.5% productivity uplift

Upside: Rapid diffusion, +3% productivity, strong wage growth

Downside: Regulation + disruption, inequality increases, slow diffusion

Policy Implications

  • AI reskilling subsidies
  • Tax reform for automation gains
  • Digital infrastructure investment
  • Labour transition insurance systems
